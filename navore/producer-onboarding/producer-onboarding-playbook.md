**Navore Market — Director of Strategy & Operations**

## 1. Overview & Purpose

### Why This Playbook Exists

Supply-side velocity is the binding constraint on Navore's marketplace growth. More producers → more variety → more consumer stickiness → more orders → more producer revenue. This playbook eliminates guesswork for Producer Specialists by codifying exactly who to recruit, how to qualify them, how to onboard them, and how to keep them.

### Who Uses It

| Audience | Use |
|---|---|
| Producer Specialists | Primary audience — follow this step-by-step |
| Director of Strategy & Operations | Review, update, enforce benchmarks |
| County Expansion Leads | Adapt Section 9 for new markets |

### The Activation Imperative

A producer who hasn't fulfilled their first order within 30 days of signup is unlikely to become a long-term partner. Every step in this playbook is designed to reduce friction and compress time-to-first-order. Target: **≤14 days from application to first fulfilled order**.

## 2. Ideal Producer Profile (ICP)

### Primary ICP: Small-to-Mid Scale Local Farmer

| Attribute | Target | Disqualifying |
|---|---|---|
| Operation size | 2–50 cultivated acres | <1 acre hobby farm; >200 acres commodity operation |
| Distance from distribution hub | ≤50 miles | >75 miles (logistics economics break) |
| Product consistency | Year-round or 3+ consecutive months | Single-week harvest only |
| Communication | Responds within 24h, owns a smartphone | No smartphone access |
| Food safety baseline | At least informal knowledge | Active health violations on record |
| Regulatory standing | Current on CDFA registration | Suspended or under active investigation |
| Motivation | Revenue diversification or primary channel | Only wants credit card payments, no ACH |

### Secondary ICP: Specialty Food Producer (Cottage Food / Value-Added)

Includes bakers, jam makers, fermenters, and specialty processors operating under California's Cottage Food Law (AB 1616) or Class B permit. Same distance and communication criteria apply.

### Green / Yellow / Red Scoring

Run any lead through this quick filter before scheduling a qualification call.

| Signal | Green (proceed) | Yellow (investigate) | Red (decline) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Location | <30 miles | 30–50 miles | >50 miles |
| Food handler cert | Current | Expired <12 months | None / revoked |
| Insurance | ≥$500k liability | $250–$500k | None |
| Product range | 5+ consistent SKUs | 2–4 SKUs | 1 SKU, seasonal |
| Prior platform experience | Yes (Local Line, Barn2Door, etc.) | Sold at farmers markets only | Never sold direct |
| Communication on first contact | Same-day reply | 48h reply | No response after 3 attempts |

## 3. Outreach & Discovery

### Priority Channels (ranked by conversion quality)

1. **Referrals from existing producers** — highest trust, shortest sales cycle. Ask every active producer quarterly: "Who else should be on Navore?"
2. **Farmers markets** — in-person, see product quality directly. Attend Saturday markets in target counties with printed one-pager.
3. **Local agricultural extension offices** — UC Cooperative Extension county offices maintain producer databases. Request referrals directly from farm advisors.
4. **California FarmLink / CDFA directories** — searchable databases of registered producers by county.
5. **Local food networks & Facebook groups** — search "[County] farmers" and "[County] local food" groups.
6. **University agriculture programs** — Cal Poly, UC Davis farm departments; connect with students running CSA-style operations.
7. **Direct mail** — County Assessor parcel data identifies agricultural properties; mail campaign for high-density ag zones.

### Outreach Scripts

**Farmers Market Introduction (30 seconds)**
> "Hi — I'm [Name] with Navore Market. We connect local producers like you directly to consumers who want farm-to-door delivery. We handle the logistics and payment — you just fill orders. We're onboarding producers in [County] right now. Can I leave you some info and follow up this week?"

**Email / DM Template**
> Subject: Selling on Navore — zero upfront cost, you set your prices
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> I'm the Producer Specialist for Navore Market — a local food delivery marketplace serving [area]. I came across your [farm/products] and think there's a great fit.
>
> Here's how it works: you list your products on Navore, we promote them to local consumers, you fulfill orders at pickup or we coordinate delivery. No upfront fees — Navore takes a commission only when you sell.
>
> I'd love to do a 20-minute call to see if it's a good fit. Would [Day] or [Day] work?
>
> [Name] | Producer Specialist | Navore Market | [Phone]

## 4. Qualification & Vetting

### Qualification Call Checklist (30 min)

Run this during the first scheduled call. Mark each item pass / flag / fail.

**Business Basics**
- [ ] Legal business name and DBA (if any)
- [ ] Primary contact name, phone, email
- [ ] Physical farm/production address (confirm within 50-mile radius)
- [ ] Product categories and estimated available SKUs
- [ ] Minimum order quantities and pack sizes
- [ ] Current selling channels (farmers markets, CSA, restaurants, wholesale)

**Regulatory & Compliance (California)**
- [ ] **Food Handler's Permit** — California requires all food handlers to complete an accredited food handler course. Confirm current status.
- [ ] **CDFA Registration** — Producers selling agricultural products must be registered with the California Department of Food and Agriculture.
- [ ] **Cottage Food Law (AB 1616)** — For value-added/specialty food producers: confirm Class A (direct-to-consumer only) vs. Class B (indirect sales OK) permit status.
- [ ] **California Homemade Food Act (AB 626)** — Microenterprise home kitchen operations: confirm permit if applicable.
- [ ] **Liability Insurance** — Minimum $500,000 general liability. Request certificate of insurance. For cottage food producers, verify any applicable exemptions.
- [ ] **Health Department Compliance** — Confirm no active violations or permit suspensions.
- [ ] **Organic Certification** — If claiming organic, confirm CDFA-accredited certifier.

**Operational Readiness**
- [ ] Reliable internet access and smartphone
- [ ] Ability to photograph products (minimum: decent phone camera)
- [ ] Packaging capability (will products be consumer-ready?)
- [ ] Order processing: can they fulfill within 48h of order placement?
- [ ] Pickup or delivery preference

**Qualification Decision**

| Outcome | Criteria | Next Step |
|---|---|---|
| **Approved** | All regulatory boxes checked OR clear path to compliance within 2 weeks | Send welcome email + onboarding link |
| **Conditional** | 1–2 compliance gaps with clear remediation timeline | Schedule follow-up in 2 weeks |
| **Declined** | Active violations, >50 miles, unable to commit to order fulfillment | Send decline email with 6-month re-application note |

## 5. Onboarding Workflow

### Timeline Target: Day 0 (Application) → Day 14 (First Order Fulfilled)

| Phase | Days | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Application & Approval | 0–2 | Producer Specialist |
| Tech Setup | 2–4 | Producer (PS assists) |
| Catalog Entry | 4–7 | Producer (PS reviews) |
| Payment Setup | 5–7 | Producer (PS confirms) |
| Training | 6–8 | PS leads |
| First Listing Live | 7–10 | PS approves |
| First Order | 10–14 | Producer fulfills |

---

### Step 1: Application & Approval (Days 0–2)

**Producer Specialist actions:**
1. Send application link (include compliance checklist as attachment)
2. Review submitted documents: food handler cert, CDFA registration, insurance COI
3. Decision within 48 hours — approved, conditional, or declined
4. Send welcome email with next-steps checklist on approval

**Producer completes:**
- Application form (business info, product categories, operating hours)
- Upload: food handler cert, CDFA registration, insurance COI

---

### Step 2: Tech Setup (Days 2–4)

**Producer Specialist actions:**
1. Create producer account in Navore platform
2. Send login credentials + mobile app download link
3. Schedule 30-minute tech walkthrough call

**Tech walkthrough call covers:**
- Account login and profile completion
- Mobile app: order notifications, order management
- Dashboard: inventory management, order history, payouts
- Setting up availability windows (when orders can be placed)

**Producer completes:**
- Download app and log in
- Complete profile: farm description, photo, story, certifications
- Set operating hours and order cutoff times

---

### Step 3: Catalog Entry (Days 4–7)

**Standards for every product listing:**

| Field | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Product name | Clear, consumer-friendly (e.g., "Sungold Cherry Tomatoes" not "Tomato Mix #3") |
| Photos | Minimum 1 hero shot, natural light, product clearly visible; no blurry or dark photos |
| Description | 2–3 sentences: what it is, how it's grown, best use |
| Price | Competitive with farmers market pricing; PS reviews against market rate |
| Unit | Clear unit of sale (per lb, per bunch, per dozen, per jar) |
| Availability | Accurate weeks/months available; flag seasonal items |
| Min/max quantity | Set realistic limits to avoid over-selling |

**Producer Specialist review checklist:**
- [ ] Photos meet quality standard
- [ ] Pricing reasonable (not 50%+ above farmers market equivalent)
- [ ] Descriptions complete and accurate
- [ ] Allergen information included for processed foods
- [ ] Seasonal availability flagged correctly

---

### Step 4: Payment Setup (Days 5–7)

**Bank account (ACH) setup:**
1. Producer enters bank account and routing number in platform
2. Platform verifies with micro-deposits (1–2 business days)
3. Confirm payout schedule with producer: [standard payout frequency]
4. Explain commission structure clearly: [Navore commission %] on each sale

**Common issues:**
- Business vs. personal account: either works, but name must match producer profile
- Credit unions: may take 3–5 days for micro-deposit verification
- Sole proprietors: personal SSN for 1099 reporting is required

---

### Step 5: Training (Days 6–8)

**30-minute training call agenda:**

1. **Order flow** (10 min) — how orders arrive, notification channels, confirmation steps
2. **Fulfillment** (10 min) — packing standards, labeling, pickup/delivery coordination
3. **Customer expectations** (5 min) — what consumers expect, how to handle substitutions, communication standards
4. **Support channels** (5 min) — who to call/text when something goes wrong, 24h response commitment

**Leave-behind resources to send after call:**
- Quick reference: order flow diagram
- Packaging guide (link/PDF)
- Producer support contact (PS direct phone + backup)

---

### Step 6: First Listing Approval (Days 7–10)

Producer Specialist reviews all listings before publishing. Use catalog review checklist from Step 3. PS approves or sends feedback with specific edits required.

**Publish only when:**
- Minimum 3 complete product listings approved
- Payment setup confirmed active
- Producer has completed training call

## 6. Activation

### Definition of "Active"

A producer is **active** when they have:
1. ✅ At least 3 live product listings
2. ✅ Fulfilled their first order without issues
3. ✅ Received at least one consumer rating (target ≥4.0)
4. ✅ Placed at least 2 orders in the first 30 days

### First-Order Support Protocol

The first order is the highest-risk moment for churn. Producer Specialist is on standby.

**Day of first order:**
- PS sends morning check-in text: "Your first Navore order arrives today — I'm available if anything comes up. [Phone]"
- PS follows up same evening: "How did the first order go? Any questions?"
- Log outcome: fulfilled / issue / no-show

**If first order issues:**
- Respond within 2 hours
- Absorb cost of any consumer compensation (Navore covers, not producer, for first order)
- Document root cause, adjust onboarding to prevent recurrence

### 30-Day Ramp Plan

| Week | PS Action | Producer Target |
|---|---|---|
| Week 1 | Daily check-in availability, first listing published | First order fulfilled |
| Week 2 | 2x check-in, review order volume | 2+ orders |
| Week 3 | Weekly check-in, prompt for additional SKUs | 3+ orders |
| Week 4 | 30-day review call | 30-day benchmark review |

**30-Day Review Call Topics:**
- Orders placed and fulfilled: on track?
- Revenue vs. expectations — adjust pricing if needed
- Product catalog — what to add for next season?
- Pain points — what's slowing them down?
- Referrals — "Who else should be on Navore?"

## 8. Ongoing Support Model

### Check-In Cadence (Post-Activation)

| Period | Frequency | Format |
|---|---|---|
| Days 1–30 | Weekly | Text or call |
| Days 30–90 | Bi-weekly | Call or in-person |
| Days 90+ | Monthly | Call; quarterly in-person |
| Issues flagged | Same day | Whatever works fastest |

### Escalation Path

| Issue | First Response | Escalation |
|---|---|---|
| Order not fulfilled | PS calls producer within 2h | Director if unresolved in 4h |
| Payment dispute | PS investigates within 24h | Director + Finance if unresolved in 48h |
| Product quality complaint | PS contacts producer + consumer | Director if pattern (2+ complaints) |
| Producer threatening to leave | PS retention call within 24h | Director for high-volume producers |
| Regulatory issue surfaced | Pause listings immediately | Director + Legal guidance |

### Community & Peer Support

- Monthly producer newsletter: platform updates, seasonal tips, success spotlights
- Quarterly producer meetup (in-person or virtual): share best practices, surface issues
- Producer Slack/WhatsApp group: peer-to-peer support, platform updates
- "Producer of the Month" feature: homepage + social spotlight for high performers

## 9. County Expansion Playbook

When entering a new county, follow this sequence:

### Phase 0: Market Assessment (Week 1–2)
- Map existing producers within 50 miles of distribution hub (use CDFA, farmers market registrations)
- Identify highest-volume farmers markets in county
- Connect with UC Cooperative Extension county advisor
- Confirm logistics coverage (delivery zones, pickup locations)

### Phase 1: Seed Producers (Weeks 3–8)
- Target minimum 5 producers before consumer launch in county
- Prioritize producer variety: vegetables + protein + specialty/value-added
- Use this playbook exactly — no shortcuts on qualification or compliance

### Phase 2: Consumer Activation (Weeks 8+)
- Soft launch to waitlist/existing consumers in county
- Use producer diversity as marketing anchor ("8 local farms, one delivery")

### Phase 3: Growth (Months 3+)
- Set county-specific producer recruitment targets quarterly
- Assign dedicated Producer Specialist if volume justifies
